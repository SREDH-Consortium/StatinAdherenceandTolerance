# Statin Adherence and Tolerance

This repository contains the code and analysis scripts associated with a PhD thesis investigating statin adherence and intolerance among patients at risk of cardiovascular disease (CVD) in Australia.

---

## Background

Statins are a group of lipid-lowering drugs commonly used as pharmacological therapy for the primary and secondary management of cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) in Australia. A major challenge in cardiovascular risk management is patient adherence to statin medication — poor adherence is associated with increased risk of CVD morbidity, mortality, and hospitalisation.

A key predictor of non-adherence is **statin intolerance**: adverse effects characterised by symptoms such as:

- Muscle pain, weakness, aches, or cramps
- Myalgia
- Myopathy
- Myositis
- Rhabdomyolysis

To improve cardiovascular outcomes, it is essential to better understand the predictors of statin adherence and intolerance, and to identify effective interventions.

---

## Research Aims

This research aimed to:

1. Identify statin-intolerant patients from electronic health records (EHRs) using electronic phenotyping algorithms.
2. Understand the predictors and influencing factors of statin adherence and intolerance.
3. Determine the interventions currently used to improve statin adherence and manage intolerance.

---

## Studies

The thesis comprised four studies:

### Study 1 — Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses
Two separate systematic reviews and meta-analyses were conducted to identify and assess the effect of interventions used to:
- Improve statin adherence, and
- Manage statin intolerance

for the **primary prevention of CVD**.

### Study 2 — Electronic Phenotyping of Statin Intolerance
Using the **ePBRN** (electronic Practice Based Research Network) dataset, this study:
- Determined the prevalence of statin intolerance in primary care
- Identified statin-intolerant patients by implementing various electronic phenotyping algorithms

### Study 3 — Predictors of Statin Adherence (Primary Prevention)
Using the ePBRN dataset, this study identified predictors of statin adherence among high-risk patients in the first two years after their date of first prescription, in the context of **primary prevention of CVD**.

### Study 4 — Predictors of Statin Intolerance and Adherence (Secondary Prevention)
Using the same ePBRN dataset, this study identified predictors of statin adherence and intolerance among high-risk patients in the first two years after their date of first prescription, in the context of **secondary prevention of CVD**.

---

## Dataset

The analyses use the **ePBRN (electronic Practice Based Research Network)** dataset, which contains de-identified electronic health records from primary care settings in Australia.

> **Note:** The ePBRN dataset is not publicly available due to privacy and ethics restrictions. Researchers interested in accessing the data should contact the ePBRN data custodians directly.

---

## Key Contributions

- **Methodological**: Demonstrated that electronic phenotyping can identify statin-intolerant patients at scale in real-world primary care settings, suggesting practical and automatic scalability.
- **Clinical**: Determined current prevalence of statin intolerance and identified predictors of both statin intolerance and adherence using real-world data.
- **Translational**: Highlighted the potential for digital, patient-centred tools for targeted interventions, connecting electronic health record systems with patient engagement platforms.
